HelpNear Child Safety Standards

Last updated: May 1, 2026

HelpNear is committed to maintaining a safe community environment and has zero tolerance for child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE), child sexual abuse material (CSAM), grooming, exploitation, or any conduct that endangers children.

1. Zero-tolerance policy

HelpNear prohibits any content, behavior, request, message, profile, review, or report that involves or promotes child sexual abuse, exploitation, grooming, trafficking, solicitation, or endangerment. Accounts or content that violate these standards may be removed, restricted, or reported to appropriate authorities.

2. Prohibited conduct

  • Sharing, requesting, distributing, or promoting CSAM.
  • Attempting to groom, exploit, solicit, or endanger a minor.
  • Using HelpNear to facilitate illegal, unsafe, or exploitative contact with minors.
  • Posting or sending content that violates child protection laws or safety standards.

3. In-app reporting and user feedback

HelpNear provides in-app reporting entry points for safety and community concerns, including reports from relevant app areas such as request details, helper details, conversations, and safety/support screens. Users may also contact HelpNear directly at helpnear.support@gmail.com.

4. Review and enforcement

Reports involving potential CSAE or CSAM are treated as high-priority safety issues. HelpNear may review reports, restrict or remove accounts or content, preserve relevant information when appropriate, and take additional action consistent with applicable law and platform safety requirements.

5. Reporting to authorities

HelpNear complies with applicable child safety laws and will report confirmed child sexual abuse material or child exploitation concerns to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) or other relevant regional, national, or law enforcement authorities when required or appropriate.

6. Child safety point of contact

The designated point of contact for child safety, CSAE, and CSAM compliance matters is:

Email: helpnear.support@gmail.com

7. Emergency disclaimer

HelpNear is not an emergency service. If a child or any person is in immediate danger, contact local emergency services or law enforcement immediately.

8. Policy updates

HelpNear may update these Child Safety Standards as the app, moderation tools, reporting workflows, or legal requirements evolve.
